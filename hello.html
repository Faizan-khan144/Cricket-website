Cricket World Cup

The ICC Cricket World Cup is the premier international championship of One Day International (ODI) cricket.

It is organized by the International Cricket Council (ICC) and contested by the national teams of the ICC Full Members and a number of other teams that qualify through the Cricket World Cup Qualifier.

The tournament has been held every four years since 1975, with the most recent edition taking place in 2023.

The World Cup is one of the most watched sporting events in the world, with millions of fans tuning in to watch their favorite teams compete for the prestigious trophy.

Over the years, the tournament has seen some of the greatest players in cricket history, including legends like Sachin Tendulkar, Brian Lara, and Sir Vivian Richards.

winners in world cup

  • Australia - 6 titles (1987, 1999, 2003, 2007, 2015, 2023)
  • India - 2 titles (1983, 2011)
  • West Indies - 2 titles (1975, 1979)
  • Pakistan - 1 title (1992)
  • Sri Lanka - 1 title (1996)
  • England - 1 title (2019)
